# Competition Checklist for Amateur Rocketry Events

## IREC DTEG Documentation Requirements

### Section 4: Vehicle Description
- [ ] **Overall Dimensions**: Length, diameter, fin span, launch rail buttons position
- [ ] **Mass Properties**: Empty mass, loaded mass, center of gravity, moment of inertia
- [ ] **Materials**: Airframe, fin, nose cone, coupler materials and specifications
- [ ] **Manufacturing Processes**: Construction techniques, bonding/adhesives, surface finish, quality control

### Section 5.2: Recovery System
- [ ] **Parachute Specifications**: Main/drogue diameters, materials, cord lengths, attachment methods, deployment altitudes, descent rate calculations
- [ ] **Deployment Mechanism**: Ejection charge sizing, altimeter model/settings, redundant systems, wiring diagrams, switch locations
- [ ] **Recovery Hardware**: Shock cord specs, swivel/quick-link specs, protective sleeve details, anchor point strength verification

### Section 6: Payload and Experiment
- [ ] **Payload Description**: Dimensions, mass, power requirements, data transmission, experimental objectives
- [ ] **Payload Integration**: Mounting method/location, accessibility for checks, isolation from vibration/acceleration, thermal management
- [ ] **Data Handling**: Storage capacity/type, sampling rates/resolution, file formats/metadata, ground station compatibility

## Test Evidence Requirements
- [ ] **Signed Procedures**: Pre-flight checklist, assembly procedures, motor installation, recovery system installation, electronics check, payload integration
- [ ] **Data Sheets**: Motor specs with burn simulation, parachute specs with descent calculations, altimeter settings/calibration, tracker frequency/power/registration, battery capacity/discharge profiles, structural material certifications/strength calculations
- [ ] **Anomaly Reports**: Ground test anomalies, flight anomalies, component failures, corrective actions, verification tests

## FRR (Flight Readiness Review) Gates
### Pre-FRT (Pre-Flight Readiness Track):
- [ ] **Vehicle Inspection**: Complete visual inspection for damage/defects
- [ ] **Motor Verification**: Correct motor installed, proper orientation
- [ ] **Recovery System**: Parachutes properly packed, ejection charges correct
- [ ] **Electronics Check**: All systems powered, frequencies set, arming switches safe
- [ ] **Payload Status**: Payload secured, data systems initialized
- [ ] **Range Safety**: Launch pad clearance, wind conditions within limits
- [ ] **Documentation**: All required paperwork completed and signed

### LFRFGO (Low Risk Flight Readiness Go/No-Go):
- [ ] **Weather Conditions**: Wind speed/direction, precipitation, lightning risk
- [ ] **Range Clearance**: Downrange area clear, recovery area accessible
- [ ] **Tracking Systems**: Ground station operational, telemetry link verified
- [ ] **Safety Briefing**: All personnel briefed on flight procedures and emergency plans
- [ ] **Final Go/No-Go Poll**: RSO, LCO, and flyer consensus
- [ ] **Launch Authority**: Final approval to proceed with countdown

## IREC-Specific Requirements
### Safety Interlocks:
- [ ] **Arming Switch**: Physical switch accessible only at launch pad
- [ ] **Continuity Check**: Resistance measurement before arming
- [ ] **Ignition Protection**: Shielded ignitor leads until launch position
- [ ] **Motor Retention**: Positive retention to prevent catastrophic failure
- [ ] **Launch Rail Guidance**: Minimum 6-foot rail for stable launch

### Environmental Considerations:
- [ ] **Weather Limits**: Maximum wind speed (typically 20 mph), no precipitation, no lightning within 10 miles, temperature range for electronics/materials
- [ ] **Site Specifics**: Launch rod orientation relative to wind, minimum distance from spectators (per motor impulse), recovery area accessibility/hazards, wildlife/vegetation protection requirements

### Documentation Package:
- [ ] **Electronic Copy**: PDF format with searchable text
- [ ] **Printed Copy**: Waterproof binder for field use
- [ ] **Version Control**: Clear revision history and dates
- [ ] **Signatures**: All required signatures present and dated
- [ ] **References**: Links to applicable rules, standards, and certifications
- [ ] **Emergency Procedures**: Contact numbers and incident response plan

## ESRA Specific Additions
### Spaceport America Cup Requirements:
- [ ] **Payload Experiment Documentation**: Detailed hypothesis and methodology
- [ ] **Environmental Impact Assessment**: For desert launch environment
- [ ] **Recovery Plan**: Detailed GPS coordinates and recovery team assignments
- [ ] **Radio Frequency Coordination**: Proof of frequency allocation
- [ ] **Insurance Documentation**: Liability coverage verification

### Technical Report Requirements:
- [ ] **Mathematical Modeling**: Simulations matching flight predictions
- [ ] **Manufacturing Drawings**: Dimensioned schematics of all components
- [ ] **Test Plan Documentation**: Ground test procedures and results
- [ ] **Failure Modes Analysis**: Potential failure points and mitigations
- [ ] **Lessons Learned**: Documentation from previous flights

## Post-Flight Requirements
### Flight Data Submission:
- [ ] **Altimeter Data**: Barometric and GPS altitude records
- [ ] **Motor Performance**: Thrust curve comparison to predictions
- [ ] **Tracking Data**: GPS flight path and landing coordinates
- [ ] **Payload Data**: Experimental results and analysis
- [ ] **Video Footage**: Onboard and ground camera recordings

### Vehicle Inspection:
- [ ] **Structural Integrity**: Check for cracks, deformations, or damage
- [ ] **Motor Casing**: Inspect for erosion or afterburn effects
- [ ] **Recovery Gear**: Parachute condition and repacking suitability
- [ ] **Electronics Status**: Check for water damage or impact effects
- [ ] **Payload Condition**: Experimental hardware status

### Reporting Timeline:
- [ ] **Immediate Post-Flight**: Safety debrief and initial observations
- [ ] **Within 24 Hours**: Preliminary flight report submission
- [ ] **Within 7 Days**: Complete data package and analysis
- [ ] **Within 30 Days**: Final report with lessons learned

## Competition Day Checklist
### Morning Preparation:
- [ ] **Vehicle Assembly**: Complete build-up at team tent
- [ ] **Mass Properties**: Final weigh-in and CG measurement
- [ ] **Electronics Check**: Battery voltages and system initialization
- [ ] **Recovery Preparation**: Parachute folding and charge preparation
- [ ] **Payload Activation**: Experiment initialization and verification

### Pre-Launch Procedures:
- [ ] **Pad Walk**: Final inspection before approach
- [ ] **Launch Rail Alignment**: Proper vehicle seating and orientation
- [ ] **Connection Verification**: Ignitor, launch controller, and safety systems
- [ ] **Clearance Verification**: Personnel and equipment positions
- [ ] **Final Arm**: RSO-supervised arming procedure

### Post-Launch Tracking:
- [ ] **Visual Tracking**: Team assignments for flight observation
- [ ] **Telemetry Monitoring**: Ground station data reception
- [ ] **RF Tracking**: Beacon signal acquisition and monitoring
- [ ] **Landing Prediction**: Impact point estimation for recovery
- [ ] **Recovery Dispatch**: Team deployment to landing zone

### Recovery Operations:
- [ ] **Location Confirmation**: GPS verification of impact point
- [ ] **Vehicle Retrieval**: Safe approach and vehicle handling
- [ ] **Initial Assessment**: Visual inspection for obvious damage
- [ ] **Data Securing**: Protection of flight records and payload data
- [ ] **Site Cleanup**: Removal of all debris and hardware

## Customization for Specific Competitions
### Team America Rocketry Challenge (TARC):
- [ ] **Altitude Target**: Specific feet AGL requirement with tolerance
- [ ] **Duration Target**: Specific flight duration requirement with tolerance
- [ ] **Egg Payload**: Raw egg integrity requirement and protection
- [ ] **Motor Restrictions**: Specific impulse and delay time limitations
- [ ] **Dimensions**: Minimum/maximum length and diameter constraints

### Student Launch Initiative (SLI/USLI):
- [ ] **Payload Experiment**: NASA-aligned scientific or engineering objective
- [ ] **Design Reviews**: Multiple milestone reviews (Preliminary, Critical, Flight Readiness)
- [ ] **Educational Engagement**: Outreach activity documentation
- [ ] **Project Planning**: Detailed budget, schedule, and risk management
- [ ] **Sustainability**: Reusability and environmental impact considerations

## Verification and Validation
### Document Completeness:
- [ ] **Check All Sections**: Every required subsection addressed
- [ ] **Verify Signatures**: All required signatures present and legible
- [ ] **Check Dates**: All documentation current and within validity period
- [ ] **Confirm References**: Applicable rules and standards correctly cited
- [ ] **Validate Calculations**: Mathematical checks on all performance predictions

### Cross-Team Review:
- [ ] **Peer Review**: Team members check each other's sections
- [ ] **Mentor Review**: Adult supervisor verification of completeness
- [ ] **Safety Review**: RSO verification of safety-critical items
- [ ] **Payload Review**: Specialist verification of experiment adequacy
- [ ] **Final Assembly Check**: Complete vehicle verification before flight

### Competition-Specific Checks:
- [ ] **Rule Compliance**: Verification against current year's rulebook
- [ ] **Class Requirements**: Specific checks for competition class (S, 30K, etc.)
- [ ] **Safety Margin**: Verification of adequate performance margins
- [ ] **Recovery Assurance**: Confirmation of safe recovery system performance
- [ ] **Legal Compliance**: FAA waiver and local regulation verification

## Maintenance and Updates
### Living Document Practices:
- [ ] **Version Control**: Increment version numbers with changes
- [ ] **Change Log**: Document what changed and why
- [ ] **Review Schedule**: Regular reviews before each flight attempt
- [ ] **Archive Previous**: Maintain history of versions for reference
- [ ] **Template Updates**: Improve based on lessons learned

### Competition Preparation Timeline:
- [ ] **60 Days Out**: Begin documentation assembly
- [ ] **30 Days Out**: First complete draft for team review
- [ ] **14 Days Out**: Mentor review and revisions
- [ ] **7 Days Out**: Pre-competition checklist validation
- [ ] **1 Day Out**: Final verification and packing
- [ ] **Competition Day**: Day-of procedures and final checks
- [ ] **Post-Event**: Lessons learned capture and template improvements

## Safety Integration Notes
### Alignment with Safety Standards:
- [ ] **NASA Power of 10**: Applied to critical documentation processes
- [ ] **MISRA C**: Principles applied to calculation verification procedures
- [ ] **BARR-C**: Guidelines followed for critical data handling
- [ ] **Redundancy**: Critical information documented in multiple locations
- [ ] **Verification**: Independent verification of all performance claims

### Risk Management:
- [ ] **Identification**: Potential failure modes documented in checklist
- [ ] **Mitigation**: Procedures to address identified risks included
- [ ] **Tracking**: Status of risk mitigation actions monitored
- [ ] **Review**: Regular reassessment of risk profile
- [ ] **Acceptance**: Clear criteria for acceptable risk levels

### Emergency Preparedness:
- [ ] **Incident Response**: Procedures documented for various failure scenarios
- [ ] **Communication Plan**: Clear chains of command and reporting
- [ ] **Medical Preparedness**: First aid availability and emergency contacts
- [ ] **Environmental Hazards**: Weather-related procedures documented
- [ ] **Post-Incident Analysis**: Root cause analysis and corrective actions
